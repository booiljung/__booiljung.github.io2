Eigenvalue Decomposition is one of the important concepts in linear algebra. Even without a deep understanding of mathematical concepts, eigenvalue decomposition is applied in various fields such as data analysis, machine learning, physics, and economics.

Eigenvalue decomposition is a tool for simplifying the analysis of complex systems. For example, it can be used to find hidden patterns or structures within data when processing or optimizing a system. This allows for easier interpretation of data and more efficient problem-solving.

This concept is particularly useful in fields like data compression, dimensionality reduction, and signal processing. Through eigenvalues and eigenvectors, it is possible to transform complex problems into more understandable forms, contributing significantly to scientific research and technological advancements.

Although eigenvalue decomposition may seem abstract, its applications are highly practical. By learning it, you will gain the ability to simplify complex systems and extract critical information.
